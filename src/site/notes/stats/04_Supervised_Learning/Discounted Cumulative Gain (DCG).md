## Definition

> [!abstract] Overview
> **Discounted Cumulative Gain (DCG)** is a measure of ranking quality. In information retrieval, it is used to measure the usefulness of a document based on its position in the result list. The premise is that highly relevant documents appearing lower in a search result list should be penalized.
